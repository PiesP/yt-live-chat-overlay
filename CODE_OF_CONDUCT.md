# Code of Conduct

To keep the **YouTube Live Chat Overlay** community welcoming and respectful, we
follow the spirit of the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/)
Code of Conduct.

This document provides a short, practical summary. If anything here is
unclear, assume the more detailed Contributor Covenant v2.1 as the baseline.

---

## Our pledge

We, as maintainers and contributors, pledge to make participation in this
project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

---

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and considerate in both public and private communication
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community and users
- Showing empathy toward other community members

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment, including doxxing or threatening behavior
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Any other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues, discussions, pull requests, and comments
- Any other project-related communication channels identified in this
  repository (for example, links in `README.md`)

---

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it using
one of the following methods:

- Open an issue on GitHub if you feel safe and comfortable doing so
- For security-sensitive or privacy-related concerns, follow the
  [Security Policy](.github/SECURITY.md)

Maintainers will review and respond to reports in good faith. Actions may
include warnings, temporary or permanent bans from project spaces, or other
measures as appropriate.
